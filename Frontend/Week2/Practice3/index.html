Biography

Fyodor Dostoevsky (1821–1881) was a Russian novelist and philosopher known for his exploration of human psychology in the troubled political, social, and spiritual atmosphere of 19th-century Russia. His works delve deeply into existential and theological themes, making him one of the most significant literary figures of all time.

Some of his major works include "Crime and Punishment," "The Brothers Karamazov," "Demons," and "Notes from Underground." Dostoevsky's novels often feature complex characters struggling with moral dilemmas, faith, and free will.

Major Works

  • Crime and Punishment (1866)
  • The Idiot (1869)
  • Demons (1872)
  • The Brothers Karamazov (1880)
  • Notes from Underground (1864)
